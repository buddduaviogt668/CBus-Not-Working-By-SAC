What's Failing Right Now

20 Years of Wear Has
Predictable Failure Points

These aren't random faults. After two decades, every C-Bus installation develops the same pattern of failures — in the same components, in the same order.

🔋
Critical

Failing Internal Power Supplies

Capacitors inside DIN-rail PSUs degrade over 15–20 years. When they fail, entire C-Bus segments can drop offline — sometimes intermittently, which is far harder to diagnose than a complete outage.

random reboots, units dropping offline, flickering on startup
🔴
Critical

Relay Contacts Welded Shut — Channels Stuck ON

After 20 years of switching cycles, relay contacts weld shut. The channel stays permanently ON regardless of commands — burning energy continuously and creating a sustained heat risk in the enclosure.

loads that won't turn off, warm relay enclosures, unexplained energy use
⚡
Serious

Relay False Triggering

Worn relay coils and degraded PCB components cause outputs to fire without a command. Lights, fans, or HVAC zones switch on randomly — especially at night or during ambient temperature changes.

loads activating unexpectedly, occupant complaints, high energy bills
🔲
Serious

Failing Switch Buttons & Keypads

C-Bus switch buttons use mechanical domes rated for 50,000–100,000 cycles. At 20 years of daily use, most are past this. Buttons stop registering, stick, or double-trigger on a single press.

buttons needing hard presses, lights not responding locally, double-toggling
🌡
Serious

Sensor Failure & False Triggering

PIR and daylight sensor lenses cloud over time and PCBs drift out of calibration. Result: constant false triggering so lights never turn off, or complete non-response leaving rooms in permanent darkness.

zones never switching off, or rooms in darkness despite occupancy
🔥
Monitor

Burnt-Out Output Channels

Channels pushed beyond rated load over years develop high-resistance contacts. They appear to function but deliver reduced voltage — causing LED drivers to flicker, overheat, or fail prematurely downstream.

LED flicker on specific zones, drivers failing more than expected
💾
Monitor

Dead Clock Batteries & Schedule Drift

Onboard CMOS batteries in C-Bus controllers last 10–15 years. With a dead battery, time-of-day logic drifts — schedules run at wrong times and astronomical clock calculations break entirely.

lighting on during closed hours, schedules 1–4 hours off
📡
Monitor

Network Bus Degradation

C-Bus cable and termination resistors develop impedance issues over two decades. Error rates climb and command response times slow — especially in sites that have had multiple retrofits over the years.

slow command response, intermittent units, comms errors in Toolkit logs

Questions

Before You Book

My system seems fine — do I still need an audit?

"Seems fine" at 20 years usually means the obvious failures haven't occurred yet — not that there are no faults developing. Welded relay contacts, degrading power supplies and failing sensors typically operate in a degraded state for months before complete failure. An audit finds them before they cause a shutdown or safety incident.

Will the audit disrupt our building operations?

No. The audit is conducted with the system live and is entirely non-invasive. We test each component in sequence with minimal disruption. Specific areas can be scheduled during low-occupancy periods if needed.

Do we have to use you for the repairs?

No obligation. The written report is yours to use however you choose — book repairs with us, get quotes from other contractors, or plan a staged replacement on your own schedule. We include cost estimates so you can make an informed decision.

How do I know if my system is in the 2006 era?

Check the manufacture date stamped on your C-Bus units inside the switchboard. If your building was built or refurbished between 2004–2010, your C-Bus system is almost certainly in the end-of-life window now.

Can you supply replacement parts if units need replacing?

Yes. We supply and install Clipsal/Schneider C-Bus components. Where older units are discontinued, we'll identify compatible modern replacements that work with your existing network without requiring a full system replacement.

What's the difference between Basic and Standard?

The Basic package is a desk-based review of your network programming, scheduling, firmware, and EOL product status — no site visit. The Standard package includes a full on-site visit where we physically test every relay, PSU, switch, and sensor on your premises.
